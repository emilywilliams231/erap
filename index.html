Available Assistance Programs

Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP)

Federally and state-funded relief that prevents displacement for low-income renters experiencing COVID-19 impacts or other documented economic hardship.

  • Past-due rent, future rent, utilities (electric, gas, water, sewer, trash), and eligible late fees.
  • Payments issued directly to landlords and utility providers; tenant pay cards are available when owners decline.
  • Income limits follow Area Median Family Income (AMFI) tables—most counties serve households at or below 80% AMFI.

Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

A revolving credit line secured by your home’s equity. Draw funds during the draw period, then repay during a fixed or variable repayment window.

  • Borrow up to a percentage of combined loan-to-value (CLTV) based on appraisal and existing liens.
  • Flexible uses: renovations, debt consolidation, tuition, emergency reserves.
  • Transparent rate structure with disclosures on variable-rate adjustments and margin caps.

ERAP Essentials

Households renting their primary residence that meet all of the following:

  • Income at or below jurisdictional AMFI limits.
  • At risk of housing instability (late rent, eviction notice, or unsafe living situation).
  • Experienced COVID-19 hardship, job loss, reduced hours, medical expenses, or rising costs that jeopardize housing.
  • Past-due rent and late fees where permitted.
  • Future rent (up to program caps) to stabilize tenancy.
  • Utilities: electric, gas, water, sewer, trash, and home energy charges.
  • Relocation costs when required to maintain safety.

Submitting a complete ERAP packet often pauses eviction proceedings where allowed by local rules. Keep communicating with your landlord, attend court hearings, and provide proof of application to request a stay.

Complete applications move to eligibility review within 2-3 business days. Payments are sent to landlords and utility providers upon verification; tenants are notified of disbursement dates.

Common delays: missing SSN, unsigned attestations, or unreadable uploads.

Landlords provide a W-9, ledger, payment instructions, and agree to waive late fees in some jurisdictions. If they decline, a tenant-direct option is documented and issued per federal guidance.

Applications may be denied for ineligibility, duplicate requests, unverifiable income, or falsified documentation. Intentional misrepresentation is subject to repayment, civil penalties, and referral to oversight agencies.

HELOC Overview

Borrow during a draw period (typically 5–10 years) with interest-only options, then repay principal and interest during a defined repayment period.

  • Minimum credit criteria with focus on payment history and utilization.
  • Combined loan-to-value (CLTV) typically capped between 80–90% based on appraisal.
  • Stable income, verifiable employment, and manageable debt-to-income (DTI) ratios.

Rates may be variable with margin, index, floors, and lifetime caps disclosed. Because the home secures the line, missed payments can lead to foreclosure.

A HELOC is a revolving line with a draw period and interest-only options, while a cash-out refinance replaces your first mortgage with a new fixed loan. HELOCs can minimize closing costs and preserve your primary mortgage rate.

Energy-efficiency upgrades, accessibility improvements, consolidation of high-rate debt, tuition support, or emergency reserves for medical expenses.

Applicants must consent to credit pulls, property valuation, flood certification, and proof of homeowners insurance. Early closure fees or annual fees may apply based on lender terms.

What to Expect

1. Submit Complete Application

Provide full legal name, full SSN, contact details, and required uploads. Incomplete packets are paused until corrected.

2. Eligibility & Verification

Case specialists verify income, arrears, and property details. Landlords and utility vendors receive contact requests within 1 business day.

3. Funding & Disclosures

ERAP payments are disbursed directly to verified payees. HELOC applicants receive disclosures, appraisal scheduling, and closing instructions.

4. Ongoing Responsibilities

Maintain accurate records, report changes in income, and respond quickly to documentation requests to avoid delays or denials.

Safeguards, Responsibilities, and Payments

Applicant Responsibilities

  • Submit full SSN, accurate household income, and all required uploads.
  • Update the agency within 10 days of income changes or new assistance.
  • Attend court dates and share proof of application to request eviction pauses.
  • Maintain copies of ledgers, notices, and payment receipts.

Landlord Responsibilities

  • Provide W-9, payment routing, and signed participation acknowledgments.
  • Confirm arrears ledgers and suspend eviction while payments are pending, where applicable.
  • Return overpayments or ineligible funds if identified in quality control reviews.

Payment & Delivery

  • Direct payments to landlords and utility providers; tenant-pay cards used if owners refuse.
  • Processing windows vary by completeness: typical review 2–14 days, disbursement aligned to provider schedules.
  • HELOC disclosures outline appraisal needs, flood certifications, and closing timelines.
